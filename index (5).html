AI for Mission Critical Communications.

9-1-1 Call Takers and Dispatchers are First Responders. On duty at 9-1-1 Public Safety Answering Points (PSAPs) and Emergency Communication Centers (ECCs) around the Country - They can us a little help dealing with Non Emergency Calls so they can focus on saving lives, limb and property in their Communities

“ AI in emergency dispatch is no longer sci-fi; it's real and it's here. ” — 9-1-1 Call Taker

The Origin Story

The Question

A mentor of mine recently challenged me, as he is want to do. Every year we recieve more and more calls at our center. How can we reduce the risk and automate processes? How would you solve this problem? Take a fresh look and get back to me with an answer.

Brain Storming

We built a toolbox of automated systems, devices, and mechanisms directed at the detection, handling and automation of non emergncy calls. We know we alone do not have all the answers. We study, listen, learn and apply what is feasible. Brainstorming, trial, error, and determination guide us.

First Steps

Our AI voice assistant is deployed to triage incoming 911 calls, quickly classifying non-emergency requests and providing automated dynamic dialog, responses, and dispatching of appropriate services with receipts by phone, sms, and/or email. This would allow human operators to focus on high-priority emergencies, improving response times and reducing call taker workload.
